Uber is now illegal in Portsmouth.

Source: Portsmouth Herald, 9/15/15

The Situation

On September 17th, Portsmouth's new transportation ordinance went into effect. Written under pressure from taxi companies, the ordinance asks ride-sharing companies to publicize sensitive driver data - which Uber has said repeatedly they cannot do. Uber has spent months trying to work with the city council, but their requests for changes have been repeatedly ignored.

"I'm sorry Uber can't work in our city.
Unfortunately the police are going to have to enforce this."

— Bob Lister, Mayor of Portsmouth

Quoted in Portsmouth Herald, 9/15/15

Taxi drivers are now patrolling the streets of Portsmouth looking for "rogue" Uber drivers. They are using the Uber app to track down drivers. They are taking pictures of license plates and calling the police. One taxi company owner requested permission to make "citizen's arrests" of Uber drivers.

Source: 'Cabbies vow to police for rogue Uber drivers', Portsmouth Herald, 9/16/15


"Taxi drivers have persuaded some city officials that instead of relaxing regulations to accommodate Uber, the city should expand regulations to cover both Uber and limousine services. If that happens, New Hampshire's hippest city will yank itself backwards in time. For Uber customers and out-of-town limo users, Portsmouth would effectively go dark. It is hard to come up with a scenario in which that would be good for Portsmouth's image or economy."

— Drew Cline, "Portsmouth's effort to regulate Uber could carry a big cost" — New Hampshire Union-Leader



Studies have shown that in cities where Uber operates, drunk driving rates decrease by more than 10%. By trying to push Uber out of Portsmouth, the city council has decided to appease a tiny but vocal minority of taxi company owners by ENDANGERING THE LIVES OF ITS RESIDENTS AND GUESTS.

They need to be stopped.
